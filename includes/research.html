Research Interests

As AI systems grow more autonomous, the security assumptions we've long relied on are quietly becoming inadequate.

Most of my work lives at the frontier of agentic AI security. As LLM-powered agents gain the ability to browse, plan, and act, they inherit a new class of vulnerabilities that traditional security thinking wasn't built for. I work on both sides of this: finding novel ways these systems can be exploited, and designing architectures that enforce trust and integrity at the points where agents reason, coordinate, and take action.

A separate thread is post-quantum cryptography. On one end, I look at how PQ protocols can secure the communication channels between agents themselves. On the other, I study how the broader transition away from classical cryptography will reshape the protocols and systems the internet has long been relying on.
